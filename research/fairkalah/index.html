Fair Mancala Without Null Capture

Supplementary Data Repository for Research Presentation

Overview

This page presents all known fair starting positions by moving 1 or 2 pieces for the Mancala (Kalah) variant without null capture. These states are not included directly in the paper due to space limitations and are instead archived here for transparency, reproducibility, and presentation use.

These positions were generated through full-state computational analysis and evaluated under the modified rule set where zero-piece captures are disabled.

For the analysis of the standard (usual) version of Mancala, please refer to the primary FairKalah research page: Prof. Todd W. Neller’s FairKalah Site .

Research Paper

Title: FairKalah: Towards Fair Mancala Play with no null capture.

Authors: Phong Pham, Todd W. Neller

Abstract:
Kalah (a.k.a. Mancala) is a two-player game of perfect information that has been a popular game for over half a century despite a strong first player advantage. Previous AI research concerns Kalah with empty captures, yet a common variation disallows empty captures. In this paper, we present the first analysis of the fairness of Kalah without empty captures, initial game states that are fair, as well as optimal play insights from analysis of optimal and suboptimal states.

Status: Manuscript in preparation
